Rift is an infrastructure-less networking stack for the Internet of Things

Infrastructure-less

Today's notion of IoT seems to imply that every device should be tethered to a WiFi network or a 3G tower. In the Rift architecture, devices communicate over direct peer-to-peer connections. Routers, gateways, and data plans are not required.

Simple and generic

The Rift architecture uses simple network primitives as the foundations of its application interface. These primitives are used solely for abstracting more complex and fine-grained network semantics into a network "language". This allows future wire[d|less] technologies to be ported and plugged in to existing applications.

Portable

As is the nature of the IoT industry, Rift is intended to be run on a multitude of devices of different CPU architectures, power profiles, and in any environment.

Good for humans, fair for businesses

The project itself aims to be inherently good for humans, and fair for businesses. We intend to keep it this way by keeping Rift implementations and designs open and community-driven. Furthermore, given the sensitive nature of IoT technologies, we aim to keep privacy concerns (data collection, user identities, behavior tracking, etc) at the forefront of the discussion.
